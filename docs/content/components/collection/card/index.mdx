A `<Card>` component is a versatile UI element used to display content in a concise, visually organized format.
It's typically presented as a rectangular box with various content elements such as images, text, buttons, and icons.
Cards are ideal for grouping related information and making it easily digestible, often used to display previews of articles, products, user profiles, and other content types.

Cards are built from four optional slots, `Card.Preview`, `Card.Header`, `Card.Body`, and `Card.Footer`, but you rarely think about them in isolation. The patterns below cover the shapes a Card actually takes in product surfaces.

## Anatomy

The `<Card>` component is a compound component made up of several sub-components that map to distinct visual areas of the card.

<CardAnatomy />

- **Card.Preview**: Optional full-bleed area at the top, typically used for images or media.
- **Card.Header**: Area for the card title and supporting labels or badges.
- **Card.Body**: Main content area for descriptive text or form elements.
- **Card.Footer**: Bottom area for actions such as buttons or metadata.

## Usage

A Card visually groups related content like an event preview, a product tile, or a user profile and sets it apart from the rest of the page with a raised shadow and border. Use a Card when the same type of item repeats in a grid or list, and each item stands on its own. If you need to mark off a named section of a page (like "Billing" or "Organizer profile"), use a [`<Panel>`](../../layout/panel) instead.

### When to use a card

Use a `<Card>` when the content is:

- **Heterogeneous.** A mix of media, headings, body text, badges, or actions that belong together as one unit (e.g. an event preview, a product summary, a venue tile).
- **Self-contained.** It can be lifted out of context and still make sense typically a summary that links to a detail view.
- **Repeating.** Several similar items live next to each other in a grid or list, each one acting as the entry point to its own thing.

If the element repeats as the user scrolls (events, products, team members), a Card is the right tool. If it owns a named region of a page, like "Organizer profile" or "Billing", reach for a [`<Panel>`](../../layout/panel) instead.

### When not to use a card

Cards are easy to over-reach for. The four scenarios below cover the most common misuses, with cleaner alternatives.

#### Don't wrap tabular data in cards

When records share the same shape and users want to scan, sort, or compare across them, a [`<Table>`](../table) reads faster and handles selection, sorting, and bulk actions out of the box. A column of cards forces the eye to jump between visual blocks instead of running down a column of values.

<GuidelineTiles>
  <Do>
    <DoDescription>
      Use a [`<Table>`](../table) when records share the same shape and users
      want to scan, sort, or compare them.
    </DoDescription>
  </Do>
  <Dont>
    <DontDescription>
      Don't render homogeneous, row-shaped data as a column of cards. Users lose
      the ability to scan and compare values at a glance.
    </DontDescription>
  </Dont>
</GuidelineTiles>

#### Don't wrap simple content in cards

A Card groups several pieces of related content. If you only have a single paragraph, a short message, or one field, the card adds visual weight without grouping anything. Use a [`<Headline>`](../../content/headline), [`<Text>`](../../content/text), or [`<SectionMessage>`](../../content/section-message) instead.

<CardVsTextMockup />

<GuidelineTiles>
  <Do>
    <DoDescription>
      Render simple, single-purpose content as plain text. Use a
      [`<SectionMessage>`](../../content/section-message) when the content is a
      status or callout.
    </DoDescription>
  </Do>
  <Dont>
    <DontDescription>
      Don't wrap a single short paragraph or one field in a card. The frame
      adds visual noise without grouping anything.
    </DontDescription>
  </Dont>
</GuidelineTiles>

#### Don't stack many cards on a page

Cards rely on contrast against the page to feel grouped. When you stack a long column of full-width cards, every row competes with every other row, the shadows pile up, and the page becomes visually noisy. Use a list separated by [`<Divider>`](../../content/divider) for similar rows, or a [`<Panel>`](../../layout/panel) when the list owns a named region of the page.

<CardVsListMockup />

<GuidelineTiles>
  <Do>
    <DoDescription>
      Use a list with [`<Divider>`](../../content/divider) between rows for
      long, homogeneous content. Wrap it in a
      [`<Panel>`](../../layout/panel) when the list owns a named region of the
      page.
    </DoDescription>
  </Do>
  <Dont>
    <DontDescription>
      Don't stack a long column of full-width cards. The repeated shadows and
      borders fragment the page and harm scannability.
    </DontDescription>
  </Dont>
</GuidelineTiles>

#### Don't nest cards inside cards

Cards already sit on a raised surface. Nesting a card inside another card creates double elevation that no longer reads as depth, it's just a frame inside a frame. Restructure the inner content with a layout primitive like [`<Stack>`](../../layout/stack), a [`<Divider>`](../../content/divider), or split the content into sibling sections.

<GuidelineTiles>
  <Do>
    <DoDescription>
      Place a Card on top of the default surface so the raised shadow is
      visible.
    </DoDescription>
  </Do>
  <Dont>
    <DontDescription>
      Don't nest a card inside another card, place it inside a
      [`<Panel>`](../../layout/panel), or sit it on any other same-elevation
      surface. The shadow has nothing to push against.
    </DontDescription>
  </Dont>
</GuidelineTiles>

### Decision checklist

Before reaching for a Card, work through these questions. If most answers are "no," consider using a simpler structure instead plain text, a list, or a section with a heading rather than a Card.

- **Is the content a complete, standalone unit?** Could you take it out of context and still understand it?
- **Does it mix several types of content?** Image, title, body, actions at least two or three.
- **Will it appear next to similar siblings in a grid or list?** Cards are designed to repeat.
- **Does the elevation help the user group related content?** If the content already groups itself with a heading or list, the card adds noise.
- **Does the card sit on a non-card surface?** Cards need contrast against the page or a sunken layer to read as raised.

If most of your answers are "no," try one of these instead:

| Need                                            | Reach for                                                                 |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Tabular records with shared shape               | [`<Table>`](../table)                                                     |
| Named region of a settings or admin page        | [`<Panel>`](../../layout/panel)                                           |
| A short paragraph or a single field             | [`<Headline>`](../../content/headline) + [`<Text>`](../../content/text)   |
| A status, callout, or feedback message          | [`<SectionMessage>`](../../content/section-message)                       |
| A list of similar rows with no media or actions | [`<Stack>`](../../layout/stack) with [`<Divider>`](../../content/divider) |

### Card with image

The fullest shape: a full-bleed image at the top, a title with supporting badges, descriptive body copy, and actions in the footer. Reach for it when each item carries strong visual identity, like an event preview, a venue tile, or a product summary.

<ComponentDemo name="card-elevation" />

<GuidelineTiles>
  <Do>
    <DoDescription>
      Put images in `Card.Preview` so they sit full-bleed at the top of the
      card.
    </DoDescription>
  </Do>
  <Dont>
    <DontDescription>
      Don't nest the card's main visual inside `Card.Body`. The padding cuts the
      image off from the card's edges and the framing falls apart.
    </DontDescription>
  </Dont>
</GuidelineTiles>

### Cards in a grid

The canonical Card use case: a collection of similar items, each acting as the entry point to its own detail view. Combine Card with [`<Tiles>`](../../layout/tiles) so the grid wraps responsively as the viewport changes.

<ComponentDemo name="card-grid" />

<GuidelineTiles>
  <Do>
    <DoDescription>
      Place a Card on top of the default surface so the raised shadow is
      visible.
    </DoDescription>
  </Do>
  <Dont>
    <DontDescription>
      Don't nest a card inside another card, place it inside a
      [`<Panel>`](../../layout/panel), or sit it on any other same-elevation
      surface. The shadow has nothing to push against.
    </DontDescription>
  </Dont>
</GuidelineTiles>

### Card without media

Not every card needs a preview. When the content is text-driven, like a plan summary, a status block, or a quick stat with actions, drop `Card.Preview` and let a title and short body do the work.

<ComponentDemo name="card-minimal" />

<GuidelineTiles>
  <Do>
    <DoDescription>
      Combine only the slots you need. Every Card slot is optional, omit the
      ones that don't carry content.
    </DoDescription>
  </Do>
</GuidelineTiles>
